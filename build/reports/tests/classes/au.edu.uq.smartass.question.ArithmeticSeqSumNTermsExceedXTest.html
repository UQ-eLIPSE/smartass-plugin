Let $2,6,10$ be the first three terms of a finite arithmetic sequence.
How many terms are needed for the sum to first exceed $1000$?
Let's solve this as an equation then round appropriately.
\begin{align*}
S_n&=\dfrac{n}{2}(2a+(n-1)d),\text{ where }d=6-2=4\text{ and }a=2.\\
\text{Therefore }1000&=\dfrac{n}{2}(2\cdot2+(n-1)\cdot4)\\
1000&=\dfrac{n}{2}(4+4n-4)\\
2000&=n(4n+0)\\
0&=4n^2+0n-2000\\
n&\approx22.36
\end{align*}
